### What is Organizational Behavior?
Organizational Behavior (OB) is the study of how people interact within groups in a workplace setting.
The main goals of OB are to revitalize organizational theory and develop a better conceptualization of organizational life.

### Key Concepts
- **Individual behavior**: Attitudes, personality, perception, learning, motivation
- **Group behavior**: Teams, communication, leadership, power, politics, conflict
- **Organizational structure**: Culture, change, stress management

### Motivation Theories
- **Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs**: Physiological, Safety, Social, Esteem, Self-actualization
- **Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory**: Hygiene factors (prevent dissatisfaction) vs Motivators (create satisfaction)
- **Theory X and Theory Y** (McGregor): X assumes employees are lazy; Y assumes employees are self-motivated
- **Expectancy Theory** (Vroom): Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence

### Leadership Styles
- **Transactional leadership**: Exchange-based, rewards and punishments
- **Transformational leadership**: Inspires and motivates through vision
- **Servant leadership**: Leader prioritizes the needs of followers
- **Laissez-faire leadership**: Minimal leader intervention

### Organizational Culture
Culture is the shared values, beliefs, and assumptions that guide behavior in an organization.
Schein's three levels: Artifacts, Espoused values, Basic assumptions.

### Conflict and Negotiation
- Functional conflict can improve performance; dysfunctional conflict harms it
- Five conflict-handling styles: Competing, Collaborating, Compromising, Avoiding, Accommodating
- Distributive bargaining: win-lose; Integrative bargaining: win-win
